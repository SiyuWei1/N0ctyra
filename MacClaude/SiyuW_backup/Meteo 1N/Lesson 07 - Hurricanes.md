# Lesson 7: Hurricanes

## Sections Overview

### 1. Classifying Tropical Cyclones
- **Tropical cyclone**: generic term for low-pressure systems forming over warm tropical/subtropical seas
- Tropical cyclones are **warm core** (warmer at center); mid-latitude cyclones are **cold core**
- Tropical cyclones rely on organized thunderstorms; mid-latitude cyclones rely on temperature gradients/fronts

**Classification by wind speed:**
- **Tropical Depression**: < 39 mph (< 34 knots); closed circulation + organized thunderstorms
- **Tropical Storm**: 39-73 mph (34-63 knots); receives a **name**
- **Hurricane**: ≥ 74 mph (≥ 64 knots)

**Other names worldwide:**
- Northwest Pacific: **Typhoon**
- Parts of Indian Ocean: **Severe Cyclonic Storm** or **Severe Tropical Cyclone**
- Super Typhoon: ≥ 150 mph (western Pacific only)

### 2. Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale
| Category | Wind Speed (mph) | Damage |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | 74-95 | Minor roof/siding damage |
| 2 | 96-110 | Extensive roof/siding damage |
| 3 (Major) | 111-129 | Devastating — major structural damage |
| 4 (Major) | 130-156 | Devastating — severe structural damage |
| 5 (Major) | ≥ 157 | Catastrophic — complete destruction |

- Categories 3-5 = **Major hurricanes**
- Scale only measures **wind** — does NOT account for storm surge, rain, or flooding

### 3. Gathering the Ingredients
- **Warm ocean water** ≥ 80°F (26.5°C) to significant depth — fuel for the storm
- **Low vertical wind shear** — allows storm to remain organized
- **Pre-existing disturbance** (tropical wave, invest) — seed for development
- **Sufficient distance from equator** — needed for Coriolis effect to induce spin
- **Moist mid-level atmosphere** — dry air is detrimental

### 4. Hurricane Structure
- **Eye**: calm center, clear skies, light winds; 20-40 miles across typically
- **Eyewall**: ring of most intense thunderstorms; strongest winds and heaviest rain
- **Rainbands**: spiral bands of thunderstorms extending outward; can produce tornadoes
- **Storm surge**: abnormal rise of water pushed ashore by hurricane winds — **#1 killer in hurricanes historically**

### 5. Hurricane Hazards
1. **Storm surge**: most dangerous; can be 20+ feet; Saffir-Simpson doesn't measure this
2. **Wind damage**: proportional to category
3. **Inland flooding**: often the deadliest hazard in modern hurricanes (rain continues far inland)
4. **Tornadoes**: spawned in outer rainbands, typically EF0-EF2
5. **Rip currents**: dangerous surf hundreds of miles from storm center

### 6. Hurricane Forecasting
- **NHC** (National Hurricane Center) in Miami — primary forecast center
- **Cone of uncertainty**: shows possible track; does NOT mean impacts are only inside the cone
- Cone represents ~2/3 chance that center will be inside; gets wider with time
- **Track forecasts** have improved significantly; **intensity forecasts** still challenging
- **Rapid intensification**: ≥30 mph increase in winds within 24 hours — hard to predict

### 7. Hurricane Naming and Season
- **Atlantic hurricane season**: June 1 – November 30 (peak: mid-August to mid-October)
- Names are on a 6-year rotating list; retired if storm is especially deadly/costly
- 11 costliest US disasters are ALL hurricanes: Katrina, Harvey, Ian, Maria, Sandy, Ida, Irma, Andrew, Ike, Ivan, Helene

### 8. Safety and Preparedness
- Know your **evacuation zone** and **evacuation route**
- Prepare a **hurricane kit**: water, food, medications, documents, flashlight, batteries
- **Board up windows** or install hurricane shutters
- If told to evacuate: **GO**. Do not wait.
- After the storm: watch for flooding, downed power lines, contaminated water

## 中文总结

**第7课：飓风**

飓风是大自然最强大的天气系统，美国历史上11次最昂贵的天气灾害全部是飓风。

**分类体系：**
- 热带低压（<39 mph）→ 热带风暴（39-73 mph，获得命名）→ 飓风（≥74 mph）
- **萨菲尔-辛普森等级**：1-5级，3级以上为"主要飓风"
- 注意：该等级只衡量风速，**不包含**风暴潮、降雨和洪水

**飓风形成条件：**
- 海水温度 ≥ 80°F（26.5°C）、低垂直风切变、预先存在的扰动、远离赤道、中层大气湿润

**飓风结构：** 风眼（中心平静）→ 眼壁（最强风和最大降雨）→ 雨带（可产生龙卷风）

**五大危害：**
1. **风暴潮**：历史上飓风第一杀手，海水被风推上岸可超过20英尺
2. **风力破坏**
3. **内陆洪水**：现代飓风中常常最致命
4. **龙卷风**：外围雨带产生
5. **离岸流**：距风暴中心数百英里外仍然危险

**预报与不确定性：**
- 不确定性锥（Cone）表示风暴中心约2/3的概率范围，不代表影响仅限于锥内
- 路径预报已大幅改善；强度预报（特别是快速增强）仍然困难

**安全要点**：了解疏散区和路线、准备飓风应急包、接到疏散命令就走。
