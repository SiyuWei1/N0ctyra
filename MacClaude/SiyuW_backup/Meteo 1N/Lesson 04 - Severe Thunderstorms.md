# Lesson 4: Severe Thunderstorms

## Sections Overview

### 1. The Scoop on "Severe"
- A thunderstorm is classified as **severe** if it produces at least ONE of:
  1. **Straight-line winds** ≥ 58 mph
  2. **Hail** ≥ 1 inch diameter (quarter-sized)
  3. A **tornado**
- ALL thunderstorms have lightning, but lightning alone does NOT make a storm "severe"
- **"When Thunder Roars, Go Indoors"** — NWS safety phrase
- Lightning kills ~20-30 people/year in US (mostly males)

### 2. Storm Prediction Center (SPC)
- Branch of NWS headquartered in Norman, Oklahoma
- Issues severe weather forecasts for **Day 1** (today), **Day 2** (tomorrow), **Day 3**
- **5-category risk scale**:
  1. **Marginal** (Level 1) — still higher than a normal day
  2. **Slight** (Level 2)
  3. **Enhanced** (Level 3)
  4. **Moderate** (Level 4)
  5. **High** (Level 5) — used infrequently; everyone must enact safety plan
- Probabilities = chance of severe weather within **25 miles of a point**

### 3. Ingredients for Severe Storms
- Three key ingredients:
  1. **Moisture** (fuel) — warm, moist air (high dew points), typically from Gulf of Mexico
  2. **Instability** (lift mechanism) — warm air near surface, cold air aloft; measured by **CAPE** (Convective Available Potential Energy)
  3. **Wind shear** — change in wind speed/direction with altitude; organizes storms and increases longevity

### 4. Types of Thunderstorms
- **Single-cell (air mass)**: short-lived (~30 min), weak, pop-up summer storms
- **Multi-cell cluster**: group of storms; new cells form as old ones weaken
- **Squall line**: organized line of storms, often ahead of cold fronts; primary threat = damaging winds
- **Supercell**: most dangerous; sustained rotating updraft (**mesocyclone**); produces most significant tornadoes, largest hail, strongest winds
  - Wind shear separates updraft and downdraft → long-lived storm

### 5. Hail
- Forms when updrafts carry raindrops into freezing air → accumulate layers of ice
- Stronger updrafts = larger hailstones
- Largest US hailstone on record: **8 inches diameter** (Vivian, SD, 2010)
- Hail causes billions in damage annually (crops, vehicles, roofs)

### 6. Damaging Winds
- **Straight-line winds** (vs. tornadic rotation)
- **Downburst**: strong downward air current hitting ground and spreading outward
  - **Microburst**: < 2.5 miles across; extremely dangerous to aircraft
  - **Macroburst**: > 2.5 miles across
- **Derecho**: widespread, long-lived windstorm associated with a fast-moving squall line; winds ≥ 58 mph along a path ≥ 250 miles

### 7. Watches vs. Warnings
- **Watch**: conditions are *favorable* for severe weather; be prepared (issued by SPC)
- **Warning**: severe weather is *imminent or occurring*; take action NOW (issued by local NWS offices)
- **Severe Thunderstorm Warning**: storm producing ≥58 mph winds or ≥1" hail
- Average **lead time** for severe thunderstorm warnings: ~20 minutes

### 8. Safety Tips
- Get indoors in a sturdy building; avoid windows
- If driving, pull over (do NOT park under overpasses)
- Mobile homes are NOT safe; seek sturdier shelter
- Have multiple ways to receive warnings (weather radio, phone alerts, TV)

## 中文总结

**第4课：强雷暴**

本课详细讲解了强雷暴的定义、类型、成因和安全措施。

**强雷暴的定义（满足任一条件）：**
- 直线风速 ≥ 58 mph
- 冰雹直径 ≥ 1英寸（硬币大小）
- 产生龙卷风

**风暴预报中心（SPC）的5级风险等级：**
1. 边缘（Marginal）→ 2. 轻微（Slight）→ 3. 增强（Enhanced）→ 4. 中等（Moderate）→ 5. 高（High）
- 即使是1级，风险也远高于普通天气日

**强风暴三要素：**
1. **水汽**（燃料）——来自墨西哥湾的暖湿空气
2. **不稳定性**（抬升机制）——地表暖空气+高空冷空气，用CAPE衡量
3. **风切变**——风速/风向随高度变化，使风暴有组织且持久

**雷暴类型：**
- 单体（短命弱风暴）→ 多单体群 → 飑线（主要威胁=破坏性风）→ **超级单体**（最危险，有旋转上升气流，产生最强龙卷风和最大冰雹）

**Watch vs Warning：**
- **Watch（警戒）**：条件有利于强天气，做好准备
- **Warning（警报）**：强天气正在发生或即将发生，立即行动！

**安全提示**：进入坚固建筑物、远离窗户、移动房屋不安全、保持多种接收预警的方式。
