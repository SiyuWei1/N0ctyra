# Lesson 8: Drought and Wildfire

## Sections Overview

### 1. A Surplus of Drought Definitions
- Over **100 definitions** of drought exist
- Course definition: **"A natural precipitation deficiency that combines with societal factors to create a water shortage and subsequent, widespread impacts to people, animals, and vegetation"**
- Drought = naturally occurring event that can be turned into a **human-made disaster**

**Three phases of drought:**
1. **Meteorological drought**: degree of dryness vs. normal; region-specific; less rain → less runoff + more evaporation
2. **Agricultural drought**: depleted soil moisture → crop stress, reduced yields; some can irrigate but it draws from dwindling water supply
3. **Societal drought**: reduced water availability → lower reservoir levels, dried wetlands, compromised habitats; trickle-down effects on food costs and ecosystem restoration lasting months/years

### 2. Drought Visualization Tools
- **US Drought Monitor** (University of Nebraska-Lincoln): weekly map
  - D0: Abnormally Dry
  - D1: Moderate Drought
  - D2: Severe Drought
  - D3: Extreme Drought
  - D4: Exceptional Drought
- Differentiates **short-term** (< 6 months, affects agriculture) vs. **long-term** (> 6 months, affects hydrology)
- **PDSI** (Palmer Drought Severity Index): uses temperature and precipitation data to estimate moisture supply/demand
- **Soil moisture maps**: show how much water is available in the soil

### 3. Drought Impacts
- Agriculture: crop failures, livestock stress, increased food prices
- Water supply: rationing, restrictions, conflicts
- Economy: billions in losses; affects energy production (hydroelectric, cooling for power plants)
- Ecosystems: wildlife habitat loss, fish kills, species migration
- Health: poor air quality (dust), mental health impacts on farmers

### 4. Wildfire Basics
- Wildfires need the **fire triangle**:
  1. **Fuel** (vegetation, dead leaves, grass)
  2. **Oxygen** (wind supplies it)
  3. **Heat** (ignition source)
- Remove any one element → fire goes out

### 5. Wildfire Causes
- Nearly **90% of wildfires are human-caused** (arson, campfires, cigarettes, power lines, equipment)
- Only ~10% are natural (lightning)
- Drought + human activity = recipe for disaster
- **Fire season**: specific time of year when conditions favor wildfires (varies by region)

### 6. Wildfire Behavior
- Influenced by: weather (wind, humidity, temperature), topography (fires spread faster uphill), fuel type and moisture content
- **Red Flag Warning**: NWS issues when weather conditions combine to create high fire danger (low humidity + strong winds + dry fuels)

### 7. Wildfire in the US
- Western states (California, Oregon, Washington, Montana, Colorado) most affected
- **WUI (Wildland-Urban Interface)**: areas where homes/buildings meet or mix with wildland vegetation — highest risk zone
- WUI has expanded dramatically as people build in fire-prone areas
- 2023 Hawaii/Maui wildfires: deadliest US wildfire in over 100 years

### 8. Wildfire Safety and Preparedness
- Create **defensible space** around your home (clear brush 30-100 feet)
- Use fire-resistant building materials
- Have an **evacuation plan** and **go bag** ready
- If told to evacuate: **leave immediately**
- **Smoke** is a health hazard even far from the fire; check air quality index (AQI)

## 中文总结

**第8课：干旱与野火**

干旱和野火经常相伴出现，但野火大多是人为引发的。

**干旱的三个阶段：**
1. **气象干旱**：降水低于正常水平，地表径流减少
2. **农业干旱**：土壤水分耗竭，作物受损，产量下降
3. **社会干旱**：水库水位下降、湿地干涸、食品价格上涨、生态系统受损

**干旱监测工具：**
- **美国干旱监测图**：D0（异常干燥）到 D4（异常干旱），每周更新
- PDSI（帕默干旱严重指数）、土壤水分图

**野火关键事实：**
- 约**90%的野火是人为引起**的（纵火、营火、烟头、电线等）
- 野火三角：燃料 + 氧气 + 热源，缺一不可
- **红旗警告（Red Flag Warning）**：低湿度 + 强风 + 干燥燃料 = 高火灾风险

**荒地-城市交界区（WUI）：**
- 房屋与野生植被交汇的区域，火灾风险最高
- 随着人们在火灾易发区建房，WUI区域急剧扩大

**安全要点：**
- 房屋周围30-100英尺创建防御空间
- 准备疏散计划和应急包
- 烟雾是远距离健康危害——关注空气质量指数（AQI）
