# Lesson 10: Winter Weather and Extreme Cold

## Sections Overview

### 1. Cold Climatology
- **January** is the coldest month for an overwhelming majority of the US (lag behind winter solstice in December)
- Some western US areas: December is coldest (2nd to January)
- Dangerously cold weather can occur October through April
- **Acclimation to cold**: takes 1-2 weeks; lose it if you travel to warm areas
  - "Snowbirds" (people who move south for winter) may actually be MORE vulnerable when cold hits their southern location
- **Cold & flu season**: peaks in February; 32 out of 41 years (1983-2024) peaked Dec-Feb
  - Cold air reduces nasal defenses; dry air keeps virus droplets airborne longer; people congregate indoors

### 2. How it Gets Cold
- **Radiational cooling**: clear skies + calm winds + long nights → rapid heat loss from ground → coldest temps near dawn
- **Cold air advection**: Arctic/Polar air masses transported southward by wind patterns
  - **Arctic air masses** originate near the poles where sun doesn't rise for months; ice/snow reflects remaining energy
  - Cold air outbreaks can plunge temperatures 30-50°F below normal

### 3. Wind Chill
- **Wind chill**: what the temperature "feels like" when wind is factored in
- Moving air accelerates heat loss from exposed skin
- **Wind Chill Chart**: combines air temperature + wind speed
- **Frostbite** can occur in minutes at very low wind chills
  - Wind chill of -20°F → frostbite in **30 minutes**
  - Wind chill of -45°F → frostbite in **10 minutes**
- **Wind Chill Warning**: dangerously cold wind chills expected (typically -35°F or colder)
- **Wind Chill Advisory**: uncomfortably cold wind chills (varies by region)

### 4. Cold-Related Health Issues
- **Hypothermia**: body temperature drops below **95°F** (normal: 98.6°F)
  - Symptoms: shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness
  - **Medical emergency** — call 911; warm the person gradually
  - Can occur at temperatures well above freezing (especially if wet)
- **Frostbite**: freezing of skin and underlying tissues
  - Affects extremities first: fingers, toes, nose, ears
  - Signs: numbness, white/grayish-yellow skin, hard/waxy feeling
  - Do NOT rub frostbitten areas; warm gradually with body heat or lukewarm water

### 5. Winter Storm Types & Snow Ratios
- **Snow ratio**: typical 10:1 (10" snow = 1" liquid); colder = fluffier (15:1, 20:1); warmer = heavy/wet (5:1, 8:1)
- **Sleet (ice pellets)**: snowflakes melt in warm layer aloft, then refreeze before reaching ground → bouncy ice pellets
- **Freezing rain**: snowflakes melt completely in warm layer aloft, remain liquid until hitting cold surface → ice coating
  - **Most dangerous precipitation type** — creates black ice, downs trees/power lines, collapses structures

### 6. Major Winter Storm Types
- **Nor'easter**: large-scale East Coast storm; strong NE winds, heavy snow/rain/ice, coastal flooding, hurricane-force winds possible
- **Alberta Clipper**: fast-moving from Alberta, Canada; moisture-starved (1-3" snow) but brings dangerous cold + wind
- **Lake-effect snow**: cold air over warm Great Lakes; snowfall rates >3"/hour possible; wind direction critical (narrow bands)
- **Blizzard**: sustained winds ≥ 35 mph + visibility ≤ 1/4 mile for ≥ 3 hours — NOT defined by snowfall amount!
- **Ground blizzard**: blizzard from wind blowing existing snow; no active precipitation
- **Bombogenesis / Bomb cyclone**: pressure drops ≥ 24 mb in 24 hours

### Notable Events
- March 1993 "Storm of the Century": 2-4 feet snow in mountains, hurricane-force winds, 300+ deaths
- "Snowvember" Buffalo 2014: 7+ feet of lake-effect snow in days
- January 1998 NE/Canada ice storm: 3"+ ice, 4 million without power, 35 deaths
- December 2022 Buffalo blizzard: 48 of 50 deaths in Erie County occurred in Buffalo; whiteout 36+ hours

### 6. Winter Weather Watches/Warnings
- **Winter Storm Watch**: significant winter weather possible in 24-48 hours
- **Winter Storm Warning**: significant winter weather expected (heavy snow, ice, or combination)
- **Blizzard Warning**: blizzard conditions expected
- **Ice Storm Warning**: significant ice accumulation expected (≥ 0.25")
- **Wind Chill Warning/Advisory**: dangerous/uncomfortable cold

### 7. Winter Safety
- **Driving**: reduce speed, increase following distance; keep emergency kit in car (blankets, flashlight, water, snacks, phone charger)
- **Home**: insulate pipes to prevent freezing; have alternate heat source; keep carbon monoxide detectors working
- **Personal**: dress in layers; cover extremities; limit time outdoors; stay dry
- **Shoveling snow**: leading cause of winter heart attacks — take breaks, don't overexert
- Avoid space heaters near flammable materials — leading cause of winter house fires

## 中文总结

**第10课：冬季天气与极端寒冷**

冬季天气（雪、冰加极端寒冷）平均每年造成约70人死亡，是美国第三大天气杀手。令人惊讶的是，死亡人数在南北各州几乎持平。

**寒冷气候学：**
- 一月是大部分美国地区最冷的月份（冬至后有滞后）
- 适应寒冷需1-2周；去温暖地区旅行后会失去适应性
- "候鸟"（南迁过冬的人）在南方遇到寒潮时可能更脆弱
- 感冒流感季节与最冷月份高度吻合（多在12月-2月达到峰值）

**风寒指数（Wind Chill）：**
- 风加速皮肤热量散失，使体感温度远低于实际气温
- 风寒-20°F → 30分钟冻伤；-45°F → 10分钟冻伤

**冷相关健康问题：**
- **低温症（Hypothermia）**：体温<95°F，可在高于冰点的温度下发生（特别是湿冷时）——医疗紧急情况
- **冻伤（Frostbite）**：皮肤和组织冻结，先影响手指、脚趾、鼻子、耳朵

**冬季降水类型：**
- 雪 → 冻雨（最危险！形成黑冰） → 冰粒（雨夹雪） → 暴风雪（≥35 mph风+雪+能见度≤1/4英里，持续≥3小时）

**安全要点：**
- 开车减速、车内备应急包
- 多层穿衣、覆盖四肢
- 铲雪是冬季心脏病发作的主要原因——注意休息
- 取暖器远离可燃物——冬季房屋火灾的主要原因
