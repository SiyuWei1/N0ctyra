# Lesson 2: Weather Data and Weather Maps

## Sections Overview

### 1. Two Types of Temperature
- **Temperature**: measure of average kinetic energy of air molecules
  - US uses **Fahrenheit**; most countries use **Celsius**
- **ASOS** (Automated Surface Observing System): primary network of surface observations in the US, maintained by FAA and NWS at most airports
- **COOP** (Cooperative Observer Program): volunteer networks filling gaps; Penn State has continuous records since **1893**
- Most official climate sites have records dating back to at least **1895**

- **Dew Point**: temperature at which water vapor condenses into liquid; also an absolute measure of water vapor in the air
  - High dew points → muggy/humid; Low dew points → dry (fire risk)
  - Dew point comfort scale:
    - < 55°F → comfortable/dry
    - 55–65°F → noticeable humidity
    - 65–70°F → uncomfortable/muggy
    - > 70°F → oppressive/dangerous

### 2. Records and Normals
- **Normals**: smoothed averages of weather data over a recent **30-year period**
- North American temperature records: highest = **134°F** (Death Valley, CA); lowest = **−81.4°F** (Snag, Yukon, Canada)
- A warmer-than-normal month ≠ every day was warmer; context matters
- Breaking a daily record vs. monthly record — monthly records are much harder to break

### 3. Wind
- Wind is the horizontal movement of air; measured in direction it comes **from**
- Key concept: Wind always blows from **high pressure to low pressure**
- **Average annual wind speeds** vary by region; Great Plains and coastal areas are windiest

### 4. Pressure and Air Masses
- **Atmospheric pressure**: weight of air above a given point
- **High pressure** → generally fair weather, sinking air
- **Low pressure** → generally stormy weather, rising air
- **Air masses**: large bodies of air with uniform temperature and humidity
  - **cP** (continental Polar) — cold, dry
  - **mP** (maritime Polar) — cold, moist
  - **cT** (continental Tropical) — hot, dry
  - **mT** (maritime Tropical) — warm, moist
  - **cA** (continental Arctic) — extremely cold, dry

### 5. Fronts
- **Front**: boundary between two different air masses
  - **Cold front**: cold air replaces warm air (blue triangles on maps)
  - **Warm front**: warm air replaces cold air (red semicircles)
  - **Stationary front**: neither air mass advancing
  - **Occluded front**: cold front catches up to warm front

### 6. Surface Weather Maps
- **Station model**: compact way to display weather data at a single location
- How to read surface weather maps with isobars, fronts, pressure systems
- Visible vs. Infrared satellite imagery:
  - **Visible**: reflects sunlight, shows cloud tops (daytime only)
  - **Infrared**: measures temperature/energy, works day and night; brighter/whiter = colder = taller clouds

## 中文总结

**第2课：气象数据与天气地图**

本课介绍了理解天气信息所需的基础变量和工具。

**两种温度：**
- **气温**：空气分子平均动能的度量，美国用华氏度
- **露点温度**：水汽凝结成液态水的温度，是衡量空气中水分含量的绝对指标。露点>70°F时人体感觉非常闷热甚至危险

**重要气象概念：**
- **气候标准值（Normals）**：30年平均值，代表一个地方的"典型"天气
- **风**：从高压吹向低压，用风的来向表示方向
- **气团**：大范围温湿度均匀的空气团（大陆极地cP、海洋热带mT等）
- **锋面**：不同气团的交界线（冷锋、暖锋、静止锋、锢囚锋）

**天气地图工具：**
- **ASOS**：美国主要的自动地面观测系统（机场）
- **地面天气图**：显示等压线、锋面、气压系统
- **卫星图像**：可见光（白天，显示云顶反射）vs 红外（全天候，颜色越亮=云越高越冷=对流越强）

**核心要点**：掌握这些基础气象变量和地图的解读能力，是在极端天气中保护自己的第一步。
