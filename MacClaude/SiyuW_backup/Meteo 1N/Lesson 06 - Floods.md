# Lesson 6: Floods

## Sections Overview

### 1. Types of Floods

#### Areal Flooding
- "Slow burn" — builds over time with fair amount of lead time
- Causes: prolonged heavy rain, wet antecedent conditions, snowmelt, predecessor rain events (PRE) ahead of tropical cyclones
- Cresting progression: small creeks (hours) → larger streams (12-24 hrs) → major rivers (days)
- Example: Hurricane Debby remnants (2024) — rain stopped Aug 8; Cape Fear River didn't crest until Aug 12
- Often called a "slow motion disaster"

#### Flash Flooding
- Much more dangerous to human life — happens "in a flash"
- Cause: extremely heavy rainfall rate overwhelming the ground (not total amount)
- Can affect **everyone**, not just those near water bodies
- Example: State College high school field flooded by just 0.25" of rain in 2 minutes
- Once flash flood water drains into rivers → can trigger areal flooding too

**Key Comparisons:**
| | Areal Floods | Flash Floods |
|---|---|---|
| Lead time | 36-72+ hours | Minutes to hours |
| Affected area | Along waterways | Anywhere |
| Duration | Days to weeks | Hours |
| Primary cause | Total rainfall + conditions | Rainfall rate |

### 2. Watches, Warnings, and Emergencies
- **Flood Watch**: conditions may combine to cause flooding; 36-72 hrs advance
- **Flood Warning**: flooding is imminent or occurring along specific waterways
- **Flash Flood Watch**: conditions favorable for flash flooding
- **Flash Flood Warning**: flash flooding imminent or occurring; **take action immediately**
- **Flash Flood Emergency**: confirmed life-threatening flash flooding in progress

### 3. Flood Fatalities and "Turn Around, Don't Drown"
- Flooding = one of the only hazards where short-term death rate EXCEEDS long-term average (getting worse)
- **#1 cause of flood deaths: driving into floodwater**
  - Just **6 inches** of fast-moving water can knock you down
  - **12 inches** can carry away a small car
  - **2 feet** can carry away most vehicles including SUVs/trucks
- **"Turn Around, Don't Drown"** — NWS campaign
- Over **50%** of flood fatalities involve vehicles
- Many victims think they can make it through; they can't see how deep the water is, especially at night

### 4. Flood Frequency & Trends
- **100-year flood**: 1% chance of occurring in any given year (NOT once per 100 years)
- **500-year flood**: 0.2% chance in any given year
- Vermont experienced **four 100-year floods** between summer 2023 and summer 2024
- Downpours increased **45% in Midwest, 60% in Northeast** since 1950s
- Philadelphia: 3"+ rain days jumped from 1-4/decade (pre-1950s) to **12 in the 2010s**
- ~400,000 more people moved INTO than out of flood-prone counties recently

### 5. Flood Insurance and Risk
- **NFIP** (National Flood Insurance Program): federal program managed by FEMA
- Standard homeowner's insurance does **NOT** cover flood damage
- Required for property owners in 100-year flood plain with a mortgage
- Average flood insurance claim: ~$40,000; 1 inch of floodwater = up to $25,000 in damages
- Uninsured owners in 100-year flood plain **cannot** receive federal disaster aid

### 6. After the Flood (5-Step Recovery)
1. **First aid** (personal well-being)
2. **Get organized** (document damages, contact insurance/FEMA)
3. **First aid for your home** (secure property, dry it out)
4. **Restoration** (restore utilities, dispose of damaged items — "when in doubt, throw it out!")
5. **Preparation** (prepare for the next flood)

### 7. Simultaneous Risks (TORFF Events)
- Tornado warning + flash flood warning can occur simultaneously
- Flash flooding generally deserves MORE attention than tornado in these events
- Supercell thunderstorms = only storm type where tornado risk exceeds flash flood risk

### 8. Urban vs. Rural Flooding
- Urban areas: more impervious surfaces → more runoff → more flash flooding
- More than 1/3 of world's landscape altered by humans in past 50 years
- Rural areas: more vulnerable to areal flooding along rivers

## 中文总结

**第6课：洪水**

本课讲解了洪水的两大类型、预警系统和安全措施。洪水是唯一短期死亡率持续高于长期平均值的天气灾害——情况在恶化。

**两种洪水类型：**
1. **区域性洪水（Areal Flooding）**：缓慢积累，有较长预警时间（36-72小时），影响河流沿线，持续数天到数周
2. **山洪/暴洪（Flash Flooding）**：突然发生，预警时间极短，可以影响任何地方，主要由极端降雨强度（而非总量）造成

**致命统计：**
- 洪水死亡的头号原因：**驾车冲入洪水**（占50%以上）
- 仅6英寸急流可以把人冲倒
- 12英寸可以冲走小汽车
- 2英尺可以冲走SUV和卡车
- **"掉头，别淹死"（Turn Around, Don't Drown）** ——绝不要试图穿越积水路面

**预警等级：**
- Watch → Warning → Emergency（紧急程度递增）
- Flash Flood Warning = 立即行动！

**洪水保险：**
- 普通房屋保险**不包含**洪水损失，必须单独购买
- NFIP（国家洪水保险计划）由FEMA管理
- 气候变化使更多地区面临洪水风险

**城市 vs 农村**：城市不透水地面多，更容易发生暴洪；农村更容易遭受河流沿线的区域性洪水。
