# Lesson 11: Climate Change and Shifting Risks

## Sections Overview

### 1. Weather Versus Climate
- **Weather** = mood (short-term, day-to-day); **Climate** = personality (long-term, what you expect)
- Climate = average of at least **30 years** of weather data
- A single warm day in winter doesn't mean climate has changed; a single cold day in summer doesn't disprove warming
- Climate influences: agriculture, water availability, ecosystems, air quality, energy, urban planning

### 2. A Changing Climate
- **Greenhouse effect**: sunlight enters atmosphere → Earth's surface absorbs and re-emits infrared radiation → greenhouse gases trap some of it → warms the planet
  - Natural greenhouse effect is essential for life (~59°F average vs. ~0°F without it)
- Key greenhouse gases: **CO₂** (carbon dioxide), **CH₄** (methane), **N₂O** (nitrous oxide), water vapor
- **CO₂ levels**: ~280 ppm pre-industrial → **~425 ppm** today (50%+ increase)
  - Highest in at least **800,000 years** (ice core data)
- Primary cause: **burning fossil fuels** (coal, oil, natural gas) + deforestation
- Credit card analogy: we keep adding CO₂ to the atmosphere "balance" faster than nature can remove it
- Global average temperature has risen ~**2°F (1.1°C)** since pre-industrial times

### 3. Shifting Risks by Hazard Type
- **Severe thunderstorms & tornadoes**: research suggests severe weather season may be starting earlier and expanding geographically; Tornado Alley may be shifting eastward
- **Flooding**: warmer atmosphere holds more moisture (~7% more per 1°C warming) → heavier rainfall events → more flash flooding; sea level rise worsens coastal flooding
- **Hurricanes**: warming oceans provide more fuel → potential for stronger hurricanes; **rapid intensification** becoming more common; slower-moving storms dump more rain
- **Drought & wildfire**: longer/more intense droughts in many regions; fire seasons growing longer; more acres burned annually
- **Extreme heat**: heat waves more frequent, longer, and more intense; nighttime temperatures rising faster than daytime → less recovery time
- **Winter weather**: fewer but potentially more intense winter storms; warming reduces snowpack → affects water supply; shorter snow season; extreme cold events can still occur (and may be influenced by Arctic warming/polar vortex disruptions)

### 4. Adaptation vs. Mitigation
- **Mitigation**: reducing greenhouse gas emissions to slow/stop climate change (transition to renewable energy, reduce fossil fuel use, reforestation)
- **Adaptation**: adjusting to current and future climate impacts (building codes, flood infrastructure, heat action plans, drought-resistant crops)
- Both are necessary — "brace for impacts already happening + work to prevent worst outcomes"

### 5. Climate Justice
- Climate change disproportionately affects vulnerable populations (connects back to Lesson 1)
- Lower-income countries and communities contribute least to emissions but face greatest impacts
- Environmental justice: fair distribution of environmental benefits and burdens

## 中文总结

**第11课：气候变化与风险转移**

本课将之前所有天气灾害放在气候变化的大背景下审视。

**天气 vs 气候：**
- 天气 = 心情（短期变化）；气候 = 性格（长期趋势）
- 气候 = 至少30年的天气数据平均值

**温室效应与全球变暖：**
- CO₂浓度从工业革命前的~280 ppm 上升到现在的**~425 ppm**（至少80万年来最高）
- 主要原因：燃烧化石燃料 + 砍伐森林
- 全球平均温度已上升约**2°F（1.1°C）**

**各类天气灾害在气候变化下的变化趋势：**
- **洪水**：更暖的大气含更多水汽（每升温1°C多7%）→ 更强暴雨 → 更多暴洪
- **飓风**：更暖的海洋提供更多能量 → 可能产生更强飓风；快速增强更常见
- **干旱与野火**：许多地区干旱更长更强；火灾季节延长
- **极端高温**：热浪更频繁、更持久、更强烈；夜间升温快于白天
- **冬季天气**：暴风雪可能更少但更强；雪季缩短；极端寒冷仍可能发生
- **龙卷风**：龙卷风走廊可能在东移

**应对策略：**
- **减缓（Mitigation）**：减少温室气体排放（可再生能源、减少化石燃料）
- **适应（Adaptation）**：调整应对方式（建筑规范、防洪设施、抗旱作物）
- 两者缺一不可

**气候正义**：脆弱群体受气候变化影响最大，但对排放贡献最小——与第1课的社会脆弱性紧密呼应。
